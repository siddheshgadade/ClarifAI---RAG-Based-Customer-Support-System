OUR DEPARTMENTS & SERVICES

1. Emergency Department:
Open 24/7, our Level II Trauma Center is equipped to handle all medical emergencies. Note: Patients are seen based on the severity of their condition (triage), not primarily by order of arrival. For non-life-threatening medical issues, consider visiting our Urgent Care Clinic located across the street.

2. Cardiology:
Led by Dr. Sarah Jenkins (Chief of Cardiology) and Dr. Michael Chen. Services include echocardiograms, stress testing, cardiac catheterization, and a dedicated Coronary Care Unit. Cardiac Rehab programs run Monday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings.

3. Maternity & Women's Health:
We offer comprehensive prenatal care, labor and delivery suites, and a Level III Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU). Tours are available every Saturday at 10:00 AM. Dr. Emily Rodriguez heads the Obstetrics unit. Lactation consultants are available seven days a week.

4. Orthopedics & Sports Medicine:
Specializing in joint replacements, sports medicine, and trauma reconstruction. Headed by Dr. James Carter. Rehabilitation and physical therapy services are available on-site on the 3rd floor.

5. Oncology Center:
Providing state-of-the-art chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and surgical oncology. Offers patient support groups (meeting every Tuesday evening) and nutritional counseling.

6. Neurology & Neurosurgery:
Comprehensive care for stroke (Certified Prime Stroke Center), epilepsy, and spinal disorders. Led by Dr. Robert Evans. The Neuro-ICU is located on the 4th floor.

7. Outpatient Surgery Center:
Located in the West Wing for minimally invasive and same-day surgical procedures. Pre-admission testing should be completed 3-5 days prior to the scheduled surgery date.

PATIENT ADMISSION & BILLING

Admission Process:
Please bring your photo ID, insurance card, and a list of your current medications. For scheduled procedures, arrive at the main registration desk on the 1st floor at least 2 hours prior to your scheduled time. Do not eat or drink anything after midnight prior to your surgery unless specifically instructed by your physician.

Insurance & Payment:
We accept most major insurance plans including BlueCross, UnitedHealth, Medicare, and Medicaid. Co-pays and deductibles are due at the time of service. If you do not have insurance or need financial assistance, please contact our financial counselors at 555-0105 to discuss our charity care program and sliding scale fees. Payment plans up to 24 months are available with zero interest.

Medical Records (MyMercy Patient Portal):
You can request copies of your medical records by submitting a signed authorization form to the Health Information Management (HIM) department. Processing typically takes 5-7 business days. 
For immediate access, use our secure online Patient Portal (MyMercy). Once registered, you can:
- View lab results and radiology reports
- Message your care team directly
- Request prescription refills
- Schedule or reschedule appointments
- Pay your hospital bill online

PATIENT RIGHTS & RESPONSIBILITIES

You have the right to:
- Receive considerate and respectful care.
- Be informed about your diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis.
- Make decisions about your care and refuse treatment.
- Have your privacy and medical information kept confidential (HIPAA compliant).
- Receive a detailed explanation of your bill.
- Request a patient advocate or ethics committee consultation.

You are responsible for:
- Providing accurate and complete information about your health.
- Following the treatment plan recommended by your care team.
- Being respectful of other patients, staff, and hospital property.
- Fulfilling financial obligations for your care.
